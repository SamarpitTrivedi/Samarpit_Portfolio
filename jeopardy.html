Retail Sales Analytics

CONCEPT

This analysis examines retail business performance through interactive dashboards that illuminate complex sales data across multiple dimensions. The visualization approach combines traditional business intelligence metrics with modern data storytelling techniques to communicate insights about revenue trends, product performance, and customer behavior patterns. By integrating multiple data views (temporal, categorical, and transactional), the dashboards reveal both macro-level business health and micro-level product insights, enabling data-driven decision making for retail management.

DATA


The dataset comprises integrated retail metrics from both e-commerce and physical store channels, including:

  • Temporal Sales Data: Daily revenue trends (Jan-Mar) comparing online vs. offline channels
  • Product Performance: 6 top-selling products with sales volume, revenue, profit, and growth trends
  • Category Analysis: Revenue distribution across 5 product categories (with some labeling inconsistencies)
  • Financial Metrics:
  • Total revenue ($2.8M)
  • Net profit ($684K)
  • Refund rate (2.4%)
  • Customer Data: 18,394 active customers
  • Payment method preferences (45% credit card)

    Key findings from exploratory analysis:
  • Electronics dominate revenue (43.8% from iPhone 14 Pro alone)
  • Digital payments account for 90% of transactions
  • All key metrics show positive MoM growth (revenue +1.25%, profit +15.8%) Two products show concerning negative trends (Samsung TV -3%, Fitness Tracker -2%)
